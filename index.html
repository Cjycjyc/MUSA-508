Here are some urban analysis report & product. Data gives us a more objective view of the city.

Does NYC Metro Region Have Home-job Balance?

NYC depends on a real estate market stretching beyond its city limits. In this project, we attempt to use data analytics and machine learning methods to build a job geography model, explore the mechanisms, and predict future trends.
Collaborators:Yinan Li, Shuting Li

Prediction for Overdose in Mesa

In 2019, approximately 50,000 deaths occurred for opioid overdose. According to one study, bystanders were present at more than one-third of opioid overdose incidents. Bystanders with the necessary tools, such as naloxone, can intervene during an overdose and potentially save a life. Our prediction will suggest new locations to set available naloxone for the public.
Collaborator: Rui Jiang

Smart Paking Philadelphia: A product that detects and visualizes street parking availability

Smart Parking Philadelphia(SPP) is a program that detects and visualizes the availability of street parking. With the help of SPP, Philadelphia can reduce congestion, pollution and traffic accidents in the center city. It will use an LED matrix to visualize current availability of street parking, and guide drivers to find parking spots easily.
Collaborator: Changhao Li, Hongyi Li, Hang Zhao
Watch the video: Link to Video

Sharing Bike Prediction

Indego is a public bicycle sharing system that serves parts of Philadelphia. By predicting the future trend, the resource allocation will be more effective.

Urban Design

Asian Culture Corridor

This work is a spatial renewal of Philadelphia's Chinatown, using the traditional Asian space, the street, as the main focus. Using the street as the space and the Chinese festivals as the sequence of spatial arrangement, we hope to activate the vitality of Chinatown in the form of an Asian Culture corridor.
Edmund N. Bacon Urban Design Competition, U.S., Second prize
Collaborator: Shuting Li, Hongyi Li

A Stage for Everyday Urbanism

in this project I started to experiment with 4D grids, and chose temporality as the fourth vector. In my renovation project of RiverfrontArtist Shipyard, added the element of time on the grid. I got the different needs of local artists and citizensemploying on-site research questionnaires and then unified the needsof different people at different times with a flexible track grid.
